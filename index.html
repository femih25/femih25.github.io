Knight Lab, Amazon Music

Designing new approaches
to social media

As a part of Northwestern's Knight Lab, my teammates and I set out to improve news consumption habits on social media -- the place where Gen Z most commonly sees the headlines.

Through extensive data analysis and over 30 user interviews, we identified three common personas:

  • The Scroller, a passive consumer who sees lots of "brain rot."
  • The Swamped, who stresses about seeing too much negative or inaccurate news.
  • The Selective, who loves social media, but doesn't actively seek out the news.

We designed one unique prototype for each persona.

  • Algorithm Shuffle, which lets Scrollers mix up their feeds by swapping with others.
  • The Gray Area, which darkens Swamped users' screens as they consume negative, inaccurate news.
  • An AI News Chatbot that sends tailored news alerts directly to Selective users' personal group chats.

Building community in
Amazon Music

During Northwestern's Bay Area Immersion Program, my team and I worked to find ways to attract Gen Z users to Amazon Music.

Our user research revealed that many preferred competing music platforms for their social features, which Amazon Music lacked. We came up with four possible ways to fill the gap:

  • Profile Badges, which let users put their music taste on display.
  • Periodic Amazon Connect events like music-based matchmaking.
  • The Mixtape, a streamlined way to make collaborative playlists.
  • A social Feed, encouraging users to see what others listen to.

We presented these prototypes to Amazon Music executives and received positive feedback for our attention to detail, creativity and dynamic demonstrations.
